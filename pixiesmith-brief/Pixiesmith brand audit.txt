It references the Rider-Waite tarot creator…
2  | BRAND ELEMENTS AND ANALYSIS — NAME

Pamela Colman Smith (1878–1951)

Early 20th-century artist, writer, and mystic. 
Created the Art-Nouveau-inspired imagery of the mythical archetypes alongside mystic and scholar A.E. Waite. 
Released the Rider-Waite deck in 1909—it is now regarded as the standard set, with more than 100 million copies in circulation. 
Deck is named after Waite, the scholar, and Rider, the manufacturer. 
Smith went wholly uncredited for her contribution despite her imagery being synonymous with tarot.  
Her claim to the deck was only cemented by her iconic serpentine signature, a monogram she created while studying Japanese design, and which she embedded into the decoration for every Tarot card.
Smith was familiarly known as Pixie, which captured her impish spirit.
Source: https://news.artnet.com/art-world/pamela-colman-smith-rider-waite-tarot-2163627

PART 4:  PHILOSOPHY

You understands the value and limitations of the word sustainability
2  | BRAND ELEMENTS AND ANALYSIS — PHILOSOPHY
Sustainability is just one piece of your broader restorative and disruptive philosophy—a shift in how things are made at the company DNA level. 
It’s about doing things the right way—not just for the world, but for ourselves. It recognizes that the life cycles of people, clothes, businesses, and the planet are all interconnected. It challenges the capitalist conception that growth and scale of a company’s success.


And you are testing a new philosophy of ethical consumption
2  | BRAND ELEMENTS AND ANALYSIS — PHILOSOPHY
You seek to cultivate a community that productively wrestles with the questions: Why does everything have to last forever? 
Designed to die means building clothing—and a business—that isn’t meant to last forever. Fashion, like everything else, is meant to evolve, decay, and regenerate.
There’s no infinite scale, no endless production. Each piece has a purpose, and when that purpose is fulfilled, the business itself will either dissolve or be reinvested into something meaningful. It’s an intentional, cyclical approach—one that challenges the idea of permanence in both fashion and entrepreneurship. 
Inspired by Patagonia, you are forging a new path: a vision of growth that is deliberate, finite, and deeply impactful.


Your Exit to Earth philosophy has several strengths…

Presents a fresh and provocative take on sustainability that avoids greenwashing and empty claims.

Lean into how your products are rare, handmade, and non-replicable rather than disposable.
Make “Designed to die” the one-liner description of your Exit to Earth philosophy and publish on values/philosophy section of website.
Incorporate philosophy into media messaging and media pitches to appeal to sustainability minded journalists. 
Weave concepts into activations (e.g. offer ways to deconstruct, return, or repurpose pieces in a way that feels ceremonial or artistic).

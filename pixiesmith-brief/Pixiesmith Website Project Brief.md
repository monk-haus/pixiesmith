**Context**

This project represents a critical step toward bringing the brand to market. After extensive foundational work defining the identity, values, and long-term vision of the company, the website becomes **the primary bridge between the brand’s internal intention and the public’s external experience. As we will be an online shopping company, and will only have pop-up showrooms in strategic points and in collaboration with designated partners.** 

Before customers physically interact with the clothing, the website will shape their first perception of the brand. It should feel not as simply a transactional platform, but a **fully immersive brand environment and experience that communicates identity, positioning, and emotional tone.**

Just as packaging extends the product experience, the website should feel like an extension of the garment itself. The experience should communicate depth, intention, and care through layout, typography, motion, pacing, and imagery.

The website must also accommodate three experience layers:

* A high-touch editorial and brand storytelling layer (Arcana collections, campaigns, philosophy)  
* A scalable, seamless e-commerce layer for everyday product sales  
* Our story/mission component

**Visitors should first feel that the brand is cool, elevated, and desirable. The aesthetic must pull them in. Once engaged, they should discover a deeper narrative about intention, quality, and community. The messaging is not the hook, it is the reinforcement.**

The goal is not to lead with explanation, but to lead with aspiration and design. The storytelling layer should reward curiosity and build loyalty.

Additionally, the system should allow for moments of elevation when needed, such as product drops, VIP access, limited releases, or special collaborations, without requiring a complete redesign.

Ultimately, the website will be the brand’s primary first impression, communicating identity, quality, and cultural positioning before a customer ever adds to cart.

**Key Principles**

At its core Pixiesmith is about *transformation*. I believe these 3 principles can be used to bring that to life:

1. Mysterious, but not occult  
   1. Experiencing the site feels like *unraveling a mystery*. The site should feel like it holds a secret — atmospheric, considered, a little withheld. But it never tips into darkness or symbolism-for-symbolism's-sake. Think quiet intrigue, not ritual.  
2. Modern, but not sterile  
   1. Clean lines and contemporary sensibility, without the coldness of tech or the blankness of fast fashion. There should be a pulse to it. Something that feels alive and intentional, not just current.  
3. Minimalist, but not silent  
   1. Restraint is the default, but the details speak. Poetic copy, considered craft, small moments of texture or surprise that reward attention. Less is more, but less is never nothing.

**Objectives**

* Translate the brand’s vision into a cohesive Shopify website that feels visually compelling from the first scroll, positioning the clothing as aspirational, distinctive, and product-led.  
* Design a recognizable and scalable digital system that reflects the brand’s positioning while supporting seamless e-commerce functionality and future product growth.  
* Create an elevated user journey that balances conversion and storytelling, ensuring the pieces remain the protagonists while the deeper brand narrative unfolds as a second layer.  
* Develop a flexible framework within Shopify that supports drops, Arcana collections, VIP moments, and long-term community expansion without requiring structural redesign.

**Deliverables**

Note: Overall, the website should be product and photography driven. People have to “dig deeper” to find the story. 

At our core, we want people to *experience* mystery and transformation. Not with "literal" visuals of mystery (e.g. occult symbols), but instead through a web experience that feels like an unraveling mystery. We are following this same principle in our packaging design. 

Provocations: How might scrolling or transition interactions offer a sense of transformation? Of unravelling a mystery? Of going deeper?
